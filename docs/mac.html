macOS 12+

macOS Setup

3 steps. No account. No terminal. No subscription.

Step 1

Download

Click the button to download OSMOzzz. The file is about 30 MB.

Download for macOS

Once downloaded, double-click the zip file to unzip it. You'll see a folder called osmozzz-macos.

Step 2

Open

Open the osmozzz-macos folder and double-click on osmozzz to launch it.

macOS security warning? If macOS says the app can't be opened, go to System Settings → Privacy & Security and click "Allow Anyway". This is normal for open-source apps not distributed through the App Store.

OSMOzzz will launch in the background and automatically open the dashboard in your browser.

Step 3

Grant Disk Access

To read your emails, messages, notes and browser history, OSMOzzz needs Full Disk Access on macOS.

1 Open System Settings (the gear icon in your Dock or Apple menu)
2 Go to Privacy & Security → Full Disk Access
3 Click the + button and select osmozzz from your Applications or the osmozzz-macos folder
4 Make sure the toggle next to osmozzz is ON (green)
Without Full Disk Access, Chrome, Safari, iMessage, Notes and Calendar will not be indexed. Files and cloud sources still work.

That's it — open the dashboard

OSMOzzz is running. Open your browser and go to:

localhost:7878

From the dashboard you can:

✓ See all your indexed sources and how many documents are indexed
✓ Search across all your data with natural language
✓ Connect cloud sources (Notion, GitHub, Slack…) by adding your token
✓ OSMOzzz is indexing your local data automatically. Chrome, Safari, iMessage, Notes, Calendar, Terminal and Files are picked up with no extra configuration.
